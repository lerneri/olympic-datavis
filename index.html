Inequality on the Podium

Exploring how socioeconomic factors influence Olympic performance

Moves Between Countries

Behind every medal, there's often a journey. This graph reveals how athletes move across borders in pursuit of better opportunities, funding, and training environments. Flows tend to concentrate toward a handful of nations, exposing an uneven global landscape where talent is developed in one place but rewarded in another. The links are colored if the move eventually resulted in an Olympic medal win.

Olympic Performance vs Socioeconomic Data

Olympic success doesn't happen in a vacuum. This view connects medals to national wealth and human development, showing a clear pattern: countries with more resources tend to dominate the podium. Yet the outliers are just as telling—nations that achieve remarkable results despite limited means, and others that underperform relative to their wealth. Together, these patterns highlight how opportunity, investment, and inequality shape who wins.


Host Advantage Analysis

Does home ground really make a difference? These heatmaps track performance across years, with host nations standing out against their own history. A recurring pattern emerges: many countries experience a surge when hosting, suggesting advantages like familiar environments, crowd support, and increased investment.
